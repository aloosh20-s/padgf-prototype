Introduction

What is PADGF?

The PreSend Adaptive Decision Guard Framework (PADGF) is a localized, client-side algorithmic prototype. It is designed to evaluate decentralized exchange (DEX) transactions against Maximal Extractable Value (MEV) vulnerabilities—specifically sandwich attacks—before they are broadcast to the public mempool.

The Problem: MEV and Sandwich Attacks

When a user submits a swap transaction on a DEX (like Uniswap), it enters a public "waiting room" known as the mempool. Predatory arbitrage bots monitor the mempool for large transactions. When they spot one, they execute a Sandwich Attack:

  1. Front-run: The bot buys the asset just before the user, artificially inflating the price.
  2. Victim Swap: The user's transaction executes at a significantly worse price (experiencing high slippage).
  3. Back-run: The bot immediately sells the asset at the inflated price, pocketing the difference as profit at the user's expense.

The Solution: Localized Simulation

PADGF prevents this by running a localized simulation against the current deterministic blockchain state. It calculates a dynamic Normalized Risk Score and routes the transaction through an autonomous Decision Engine. The transaction is then either Executed, Delayed, or Blocked—preventing the user's capital from ever being exposed to predators.

Part 1: End-User Guide

This section is for the everyday user or trader utilizing a wallet or application integrated with PADGF.

How PADGF Protects You

Traditionally, when you click "Swap", your transaction goes straight to the blockchain, hoping for the best. With PADGF integrated:

  1. You initiate a swap.
  2. PADGF pauses. It secretly simulates your exact transaction on a private, localized copy of the blockchain.
  3. PADGF calculates the risk. It checks if your transaction is profitable enough for an attacker to sandwich.
  4. PADGF makes a decision:
    • Execute (Green): The network conditions are safe. Your swap goes through immediately.
    • Delay (Yellow): There is moderate risk. PADGF holds the transaction, waiting for safer network conditions.
    • Block (Red): The risk is critically high. PADGF stops the transaction to save your funds.

Using the PADGF Dashboard

If you are evaluating PADGF via its prototype dashboard:

  1. Double-click the Run-PADGF-Dashboard.bat file.
  2. Open your web browser and navigate to http://localhost:3000.
  3. You will see an evaluation matrix with intuitive visual graphs mapping the expected output (Baseline) vs. the Attacked output vs. the Protected output.

Interpreting Risk

PADGF uses factors like your set slippage, the current gas price, and the expected price impact to generate a final risk score. You don't need to know the math; you simply trust the Engine's color-coded output. If your transaction is Blocked, simply try again with a tighter slippage tolerance or wait for network volatility to decrease.

Part 2: Provider and Developer Guide

This section is intended for wallet providers, node operators, researchers, and developers integrating or extending the PADGF architecture.

Architecture Overview

PADGF operates entirely client-side (or on the provider's backend) prior to broadcasting to the public mempool. It uses a deterministic mainnet fork (via Hardhat) to achieve perfect state-isolation for its simulations.

Core Modules

The logic is cleanly separated into modular components located in the /src directory:

  1. Provider Setup (providerSetup.js)
    • Handles the Hardhat network state. To ensure deterministic testing, it impersonates a whale account and funds it with simulated ETH to cover gas costs.
  2. DEX Interaction (dexInteraction.js)
    • Manages on-chain communication with the Uniswap V2 Router and ERC20 tokens.
    • Fetches quotes (getAmountsOut) to establish pristine baselines.
    • Calculates gas fees dynamically and handles transaction execution limits.
  3. Risk Evaluator (riskEvaluator.js)
    • The mathematical engine. It calculates:
      • Slippage Deviation: Difference between expected and simulated outputs.
      • Attacker Net Profit: Evaluates the extracted value minus standard pool fees, builder bribes (e.g., 90%), and network gas costs.
    • Returns a Normalized Risk Score (R) between 0.0 and 1.0.
  4. Decision Engine (decisionEngine.js)
    • Evaluates the Risk Score against configurable thresholds (Tau 1 and Tau 2).
    • R < Tau 1 (e.g., 0.3) -> Execute
    • Tau 1 <= R < Tau 2 (e.g., 0.7) -> Delay
    • R >= Tau 2 -> Block

The Mathematical Model

The framework relies on a normalized function: R = (w1 * Normalized Profitability) + (w2 * Normalized Slippage)

Where w1 and w2 are adjustable weights based on the provider's risk appetite. By converting raw ETH values and slippage percentages into standardized 0.0 - 1.0 bounds, PADGF can universally evaluate any trade size objectively.

Experimental Execution Flows

For research and validation, PADGF relies on three strict phases located in /scripts:

  1. Phase 1: Baseline Swaps (baseline-swap.js)
    • Establishes a ground-truth benchmark with zero interference.
  2. Phase 2: MEV Attack Simulation (sandwich-attack.js)
    • Proves vulnerability. An attacker front-runs the victim (inflating price), the victim swaps (absorbing damage), and the attacker back-runs (profiting).
  3. Phase 3: Protected Swaps (protected-swap.js)
    • The core pipeline. Simulates the swap via eth_call without broadcasting, evaluates the risk, and autonomously decides whether to proceed with executeSwap().

Integration Guidelines

If extending PADGF for a production environment:

  • Custom Risk Models: Adjust w1 and w2 inside src/riskEvaluator.js, or incorporate historical pool volatility.
  • DEX Support: Extend src/dexInteraction.js to support Uniswap V3 or Curve routing.
  • Automated Delay Queues: Expand src/decisionEngine.js to place Delayed transactions into a Redis queue, polling mempool conditions every block until risk drops below the acceptable threshold.

Conclusion

The PreSend Adaptive Decision Guard Framework (PADGF) represents a proactive paradigm shift in decentralized finance security. Instead of reacting to attacks or relying solely on private RPCs, PADGF empowers end-users and providers with algorithmic, pre-broadcast simulation and automated decision-making. By implementing PADGF, providers can guarantee capital safety and eliminate sandwich attack vulnerabilities entirely.
