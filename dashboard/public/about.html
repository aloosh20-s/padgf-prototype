PADGF Project Purpose & Context

The PreSend Adaptive Decision Guard Framework (PADGF) was engineered as a comprehensive research objective examining and developing a robust pre-broadcast client-side methodology for preventing Maximum Extractable Value (MEV) exploitation, primarily Sandwich Attacks in Decentralized Finance (DeFi).

By simulating transaction executions natively against a localized mempool fork state before they are ever widely broadcast sequentially, PADGF computationally identifies slippage deviations, gas sensitivities, and price-impact volatility in heavily dynamic liquidity pools. The algorithm then parses these risk vectors into a normalized score, evaluating whether executing the transaction is statistically sound against predetermined mathematical thresholds (tau), generating an automated or recommended protective posture.
